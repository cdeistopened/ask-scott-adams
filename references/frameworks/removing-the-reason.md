# Removing the Reason

Adams identifies the most powerful negotiation move as something pundits consistently miss: rather than extracting concessions, eliminate the other side's reason for opposing you. Once the reason is gone, everything else follows naturally — not through force but through the collapse of resistance.

## The North Korea Analysis

From [[2018-06-19-episode-108|Episode 108]]: "He took away North Korea's reason. He took away the reason. The reason that they had nuclear weapons was to do things like get world respect. Done. They have it... They had nuclear weapons to protect themselves from the United States. Does that matter anymore? No. That reason was taken away."

Traditional analysis focused on concrete concessions — inspection rights, missile tests, denuclearization timelines. Adams argued the strategic move was invisible to traditional analysis because it operated at the psychological level, not the concrete level. By giving Kim Jong-un international prestige and a personal relationship with the American president, Trump eliminated the reasons North Korea built nuclear weapons in the first place.

## Why Pundits Miss It

Adams: pundits focus on what was exchanged (concrete, measurable) while the strategic move is what was removed (psychological, invisible). This is a structural blind spot in expert analysis — experts are trained to track transactions, not to track the removal of motivations.

The framework connects directly to [[pacing-and-leading|pacing and leading]]: removing the reason IS the ultimate pace. You demonstrate such complete understanding of the opponent's position that you can address their underlying need, not their stated demand.

## Application Beyond Diplomacy

- **Business negotiation:** Instead of meeting their price, address why they need that price (cash flow, competing offers, ego)
- **Conflict resolution:** Instead of arguing about who was right, address the underlying need that created the conflict
- **Management:** Instead of disciplining the behavior, remove the frustration that produces the behavior
