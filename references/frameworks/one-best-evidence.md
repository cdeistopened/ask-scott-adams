# One Best Evidence

Adams's diagnostic technique for cutting through laundry list arguments. When someone presents a long list of accusations or evidence — each item individually weak — and claims the aggregate is overwhelming, Adams applies a simple test: "What is your single best piece of evidence?"

## The "10 x 0 = Banana" Principle

From [[2018-11-03-episode-284|Episode 284]]: ten pieces of bad evidence don't add up to one piece of good evidence. "10 x 0 = Banana" — multiplying zeros gives you nothing, no matter how many you have. A laundry list of weak arguments feels persuasive because of volume, not because of substance.

## The Challenge Technique

When faced with a laundry list:
1. Ask: "What is your single best piece of evidence?"
2. Evaluate that ONE piece on its own merits
3. If the single best piece doesn't hold up, the entire list is worthless — it's all weaker than the best

Adams: "If you can't describe it succinctly, you don't understand it." The laundry list is a tell for cognitive dissonance — when someone needs five reasons where one strong reason would suffice, none of the five is actually the reason.

## Connection to the Laundry List Tell

This framework operationalizes what Adams identifies in his [[cognitive-dissonance|cognitive dissonance]] diagnostic as the "laundry list tell" — when someone stacks five weak complaints because no single one is strong enough. The One Best Evidence challenge is the counter-technique: force the stacker to commit to their strongest claim, then evaluate it alone.
