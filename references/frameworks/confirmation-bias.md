# Confirmation Bias

Adams treats confirmation bias not as a footnote in the cognitive bias catalog but as the master mechanism that maintains every other cognitive trap. It is what keeps the [[two-movies-on-one-screen|two movies]] running on parallel tracks. It is what prevents [[cognitive-dissonance|cognitive dissonance]] from self-correcting. It is why [[loserthink|loserthink]] persists even when contrary evidence is freely available.

His formulation: humans do not process information and then form beliefs. Humans hold beliefs and then filter information to confirm them. Evidence that supports the belief is noticed, remembered, and weighted heavily. Evidence that contradicts the belief is ignored, forgotten, or reinterpreted as actually confirming.

The implication for the [[persuasion-filter|persuasion filter]]: facts do not change minds because facts are processed through the confirmation bias filter before they reach the mind. Only persuasion techniques that bypass the filter — visual imagery, emotional resonance, identity shifts — can produce genuine belief change.

## Adams's Test

Adams's practical diagnostic: "If you think only the other side has confirmation bias and your side is seeing reality clearly, that is your confirmation bias talking."

Both movies on one screen are maintained by confirmation bias. Each side sees exactly the evidence that supports their movie and dismisses exactly the evidence that supports the other movie. The mechanism is symmetric — which is why Adams insists that prediction tracking, not evidence evaluation, is the only reliable arbiter.
