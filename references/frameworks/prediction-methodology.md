# Prediction Methodology

Adams does not claim to predict events through superior information or analytical rigor. He claims to predict through a specific lens — the [[persuasion-filter|persuasion filter]] — that surfaces patterns invisible to analysts using the rational filter.

## The Method

Adams's prediction process, as described across multiple episodes:

1. **Identify the persuasion dynamics.** Who is using which techniques? What is the relative persuasion skill of each party?
2. **Assess the persuasion gap.** How large is the difference in persuasion effectiveness between the competing parties?
3. **Predict based on persuasion advantage.** The party with the stronger persuasion stack wins, regardless of who has better facts or policies.
4. **Track the prediction publicly.** Adams insists on public, time-stamped predictions with clear success criteria.
5. **Update the model based on results.** If the prediction fails, examine whether the filter failed or an external variable (black swan) intervened.

Adams insists on a specific accountability mechanism: the prediction must be public, time-stamped, and have clear success criteria. From Episode 89: he repeatedly tests the persuasion filter against the "facts matter" filter and the "Trump is lucky" filter by tracking whose predictions come true.

## What Makes This Different

Most prediction methodologies rely on data analysis, expert consensus, or statistical modeling. Adams's method relies on identifying the dominant persuasion technique and predicting its effect on public behavior.

His argument for this approach: data-driven predictions assume rational actors responding to facts. If humans are [[moist-robot|moist robots]] responding to persuasion inputs, data about facts is less predictive than analysis of persuasion inputs.

## Known Limitations

Adams acknowledges two major failure modes:

1. **Black swans.** The persuasion filter cannot predict exogenous shocks (COVID, natural disasters, scandals) that restructure the landscape.
2. **Institutional override.** Individual persuasion skill can be overwhelmed by coordinated institutional opposition — a lesson Adams learned from Trump's 2020 experience.

## Track Record

Adams's claimed hits: 2016 election, North Korea talks, economic trajectory under Trump, "Sleepy Joe" as an effective kill shot.

Adams's acknowledged misses: 2020 "landslide" prediction (pre-COVID), various specific predictions where timing was wrong.

The honest assessment: Adams's filter appears to have genuine predictive power in stable political environments and genuine blind spots regarding exogenous disruption.
