# Pattern Recognition

Adams calls pattern recognition "the superpower and the glitch" — the same cognitive ability that makes humans intelligent also makes them hallucinate patterns that don't exist.

## The Superpower

Pattern recognition is how Adams explains his own prediction success. He does not claim to have better information than other analysts. He claims to recognize persuasion patterns that others miss because they are looking for factual patterns instead.

His 2016 election call was a pattern recognition exercise: he recognized Trump's communication as matching patterns from his hypnosis training — pacing and leading, anchoring, visual persuasion, identity play — and concluded that these patterns predicted success regardless of policy substance.

## The Glitch

The same ability that finds real patterns also finds false ones. Adams identifies this as one of the primary drivers of [[confirmation-bias|confirmation bias]], [[mass-hysteria|mass hysteria]], and conspiracy thinking.

Adams's diagnostic: "If the pattern you're seeing perfectly confirms your existing worldview and perfectly condemns the people you already don't like, you should be suspicious that you're hallucinating the pattern."

The problem: real patterns and hallucinated patterns FEEL identical from the inside. There is no internal signal that distinguishes between genuine insight and pattern-matching gone wrong.

Adams's proposed fix: track predictions. A genuine pattern produces accurate predictions about what happens next. A hallucinated pattern does not. Prediction tracking is the only reliable way to distinguish between the two.
