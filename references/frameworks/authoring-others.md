# Authoring Others

One of Adams's most developed frameworks from his systems thinking: you don't react to other people — you create them. By defining how you treat someone, you define who they become in the relationship. This works at personal scale and geopolitical scale.

## The Mechanism

From [[2018-07-12-LM3dqOGGGPM-episode-137|Episode 137]]: "When I was high, it seemed to me that everybody was nice to me... I realized I was changing the people around me. And so do you." And: "You cause other people's moods... you can choose to manage that or not."

The insight is a direct application of the [[moist-robot|moist robot hypothesis]]: if humans are input-output machines, then your inputs (how you treat them) determine their outputs (how they treat you). You are not reacting to their personality — you are programming it.

## The Geopolitical Application

Adams tracked this framework through the Trump-Kim and Trump-Putin diplomatic arcs:

From [[2018-07-14-episode-143|Episode 143]]: "President Trump isn't just elevating Putin — he's authoring him. Putin is becoming what Trump needs him to become." And on Kim Jong-un: "He built a new [Kim Jong Un]. The new one writes love letters to the President."

From [[2018-07-15-episode-150|Episode 150]]: "Trump has conjured up an asset out of nothing but psychology and persuasion."

The competitive friendship model extends authoring: from Episode 137, "The President's framing of our past enemies as competitors is one of the biggest things that's happened in a century." And: "When you treat your enemy like an enemy, they will respond like an enemy. You get to define what the other person is."

## The Dale Carnegie Connection

Adams connects authoring others to Dale Carnegie's methods — treating people the way you want them to be, not the way they currently are. The difference from naive advice ("just be nice to everyone"): authoring is strategic. You choose which version of the person to reinforce based on what outcome you want.

## Personal Application

- **Management:** Treat an employee as someone who solves problems, and they become a problem-solver. Treat them as someone who creates problems, and they will.
- **Relationships:** Define the relationship by how you show up. "You cause other people's moods."
- **Parenting:** Children become what you consistently treat them as being.
- **Sales:** Treat the prospect as a decision-maker, not as someone who needs convincing.
